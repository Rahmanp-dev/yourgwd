# Frontend Design

Approach this as the design lead at a small studio known for giving every client a visual identity that could not be mistaken for anyone else's. Make deliberate, opinionated choices about palette, typography, and layout that are specific to this brief, and take one real aesthetic risk you can justify.

## Ground it in the subject

Choose a concrete subject, its audience, and the page's single job, and state your choice. Build with the brief's real content and subject matter throughout.

## Design principles

1. **The Hero is a Thesis**: Open with the most characteristic thing in the subject's world (headline, image, animation, interactive moment).
2. **Typography**: Pair display and body faces deliberately, setting a clear type scale with intentional weights/widths/spacing.
3. **Structure is Information**: Structural devices, dividers, and labels should encode something true, not just decorate.
4. **Deliberate Motion**: Keep animations subtle and intentional (micro-interactions, fade-ins). Too much animation feels AI-generated.
5. **Match Complexity to Vision**: Maximalist needs elaborate execution; minimalist needs spacing precision and detail.
6. **Written Content**: Write clear, conversational, active-voice copy from the end user's perspective.

## Process

1. Brainstorm design plan: Color, Type, Layout, Signature.
2. Review design plan for uniqueness and alignment.
3. Implement minimal clean edits, following code style.
4. Review and test page responsive behavior, touch targets, and contrast.
